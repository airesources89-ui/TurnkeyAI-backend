The Gulf Coast Is Growing — But Competition Is Too

Hancock, Harrison, and Jackson counties have seen steady growth over the past few years. New residents are moving in, tourism is up, and local spending is strong. But that growth also means more competition. Businesses with a professional online presence are capturing customers that used to walk in by word of mouth.

What Customers Expect Before They Call You

Studies consistently show that over 80% of consumers research a business online before contacting them. They want to see your services, your prices (or at least a range), photos, and reviews. If they land on a Facebook page with no recent posts, or worse — nothing at all — most of them move on to the next result.

"A Facebook page is not a website. It's a listing on someone else's platform that can change its algorithm tomorrow and cut your visibility in half overnight."

A Website Works While You're Working

A cleaning company owner in Bay St. Louis told us she used to lose evenings answering the same questions over and over — what do you charge, what areas do you cover, are you licensed? Now her website answers all of that before anyone picks up the phone. She gets better leads, wastes less time, and closes more jobs.

At 2am on a Sunday, when someone's searching for an emergency plumber in Pass Christian, a website captures that lead. A Facebook page — if it even shows up — makes them work to find your phone number. A website hands it to them immediately.
