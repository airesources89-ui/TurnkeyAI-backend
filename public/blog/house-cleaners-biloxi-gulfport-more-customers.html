House Cleaners in Biloxi and Gulfport:
Get More Customers Without Paying for Ads

📅 Week 2 of 12 ⏱ 4 min read 📍 Gulf Coast, MS

You started your cleaning business because you're good at it. You know how to make a house shine, how to earn repeat customers, and how to manage a schedule. But if you're like most independent cleaners on the Gulf Coast, finding new customers means posting in Facebook groups, hoping for referrals, or paying for lead services that send the same lead to five other cleaners at the same time.

There's a better way. And it doesn't cost you anything in ad spend.

The Problem With Facebook Groups and Lead Services

Facebook groups like "Bay St. Louis Neighbors" or "Biloxi Community Board" can generate leads. But they come with real downsides. You're competing with every other cleaner who posts in the same group. Your post disappears within hours as new posts push it down. And you're building your business on a platform you don't control — Facebook can change its algorithm, restrict business posts, or shut down a group tomorrow.

Lead services like Thumbtack and Angi have a different problem. They sell the same lead to multiple cleaners. By the time you call, the customer has already heard from three other people. You're competing on price before you've even had a chance to show what makes you different.

"When five cleaners call the same person within ten minutes, the only thing that matters is who quotes the lowest price. That's a race to the bottom — and nobody wins that race."

What Actually Works: Being the First Result When Someone Searches

When someone in Gulfport types "house cleaner near me" into their phone, Google shows them a list of results. If you have a website with your services, your area, your phone number, and a few reviews mentioned on it — you show up. If you don't have a website, you don't exist in that search. Period.

The people searching on Google aren't browsing. They've already decided they need a cleaner. They're ready to hire someone today. That's the highest-quality lead you can get — someone who came looking for you, not someone you had to chase down in a Facebook comment thread.

What Your Website Needs to Say

You don't need a fancy website. You need a clear one. Here's what a potential customer wants to know before they pick up the phone:

What do you clean? Houses, apartments, offices, move-in/move-out, deep cleans, regular weekly or biweekly service. List it plainly.

Where do you work? Biloxi, Gulfport, Long Beach, Pass Christian, Bay St. Louis, Ocean Springs — name every city and neighborhood you serve. This is what Google uses to match you with local searches.

How do they reach you? A phone number at the top of the page, big enough to tap on a phone screen. A simple booking form. That's it.

Can they trust you? Licensed and insured. Years in business. A few words about who you are and why you care about doing good work. People hire people — especially when those people are coming into their homes.
