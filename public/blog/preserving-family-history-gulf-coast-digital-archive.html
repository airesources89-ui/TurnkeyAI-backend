Preserving Family History on the Gulf Coast:
The Digital Archive Every Family Should Build

📷 Week 10 of 12 · 4 min read · Gulf Coast, MS

Gulf Coast families have deep roots. Some go back generations in Hancock County or Harrison County, with stories of fishing, farming, surviving hurricanes, and building communities from scratch. Those stories — and the photos, documents, and memories that go with them — are irreplaceable. And they're disappearing.

What Gets Lost When It Isn't Captured

Old photo albums deteriorate. The elder who remembers the stories passes away. The recipes exist only in someone's head. The property documents get lost in a flood. Every year, families across the Gulf Coast lose pieces of their history that can never be recovered. A digital archive changes that.

What a Family History Website Preserves

A TurnkeyAI family site can include scanned photographs with captions and dates, family tree and genealogy information, written stories and oral histories, videos and audio recordings, important documents, and a timeline of significant family events. Everything in one place, accessible by every member of the family from anywhere.

Private, Secure, and Permanent

Unlike Facebook, where your family's memories live on someone else's platform and could disappear at any time, your family website is yours. Password-protected so only family members can access it. Hosted reliably. And there as long as you want it.

A Gift to Future Generations

Imagine your grandchildren 30 years from now being able to read your grandfather's story in his own words, see photos of the farm before Camille hit, or learn the original recipe for the gumbo served at every family reunion. That's what a family website makes possible.
