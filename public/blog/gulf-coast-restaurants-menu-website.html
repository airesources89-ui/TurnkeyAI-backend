Restaurants and Food Businesses on the Gulf Coast:
Your Menu Belongs Online

🦐 Week 11 of 12 · 3 min read · Gulf Coast, MS

Someone visiting Biloxi for the weekend wants to find a great local seafood spot for dinner. They pull out their phone, search, and start clicking. If your restaurant doesn't have a website — or has one that looks outdated and doesn't load well on mobile — they're clicking past you to whoever is next on the list.

Your Menu Needs to Be Findable

Menus should not exist only on paper or on a Facebook post from three years ago. Customers want to see your menu before they decide to visit. Google wants to index your menu so it can show your restaurant when someone searches 'seafood Gulfport' or 'best gumbo Pass Christian.' A proper website with your full menu makes both happen.

Tourism Is Your Opportunity

The Gulf Coast draws millions of visitors each year — to the casinos, the beaches, the music festivals, and the food scene. Tourists search for restaurants online almost universally. A well-optimized website makes your restaurant visible to visitors who would never have found you otherwise, bringing in business beyond your regular customer base.

Hours, Location, and Specials — Updated and Accurate

Nothing frustrates a customer more than driving across town to a restaurant that closed two hours early or is running different hours than what's listed online. Your website is the authoritative source for your hours, location, and specials. When it's accurate and up to date, customers trust you — and come back.
