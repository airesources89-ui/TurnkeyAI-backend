5 Website Mistakes
Gulf Coast Businesses Make

Week 2 of 12 5 min read Gulf Coast, MS

Having a website is step one. But having a bad website can actually be worse than having no website at all. A site that loads slowly, looks outdated, or confuses visitors sends a clear message: this business doesn't have its act together.

We've reviewed hundreds of small business websites across the Mississippi Gulf Coast — from Bay St. Louis to Pascagoula — and the same five mistakes show up over and over. The good news? Every one of them is fixable.

1. No Phone Number Above the Fold

When someone lands on your website from a Google search, they're usually ready to take action. They want to call you. If they have to scroll, hunt through menus, or click a "Contact" page to find your phone number, a percentage of them will just hit the back button and call the next business instead.

The fix: Your phone number should be visible the moment the page loads — ideally as a clickable tap-to-call button on mobile. Every TurnkeyAI site puts the phone number front and center in the hero section and in a sticky header.

2. The Site Isn't Mobile-Friendly

Over 70% of local searches happen on a phone. If your website was built on a desktop five years ago and nobody ever checked how it looks on an iPhone, you're in trouble. Text that's too small to read, buttons you can't tap, and layouts that require sideways scrolling all scream "unprofessional."

The fix: Your site needs to be built mobile-first. That means it's designed for the phone screen first, and then scales up for desktop — not the other way around. Every element should be tappable, readable, and fast on a cell connection.

3. No Clear Call to Action

A surprising number of business websites are essentially digital brochures. They list services, maybe show a few photos, and that's it. There's no "Book Now" button, no "Get a Free Estimate" form, no reason for the visitor to do anything except leave.

The fix: Every page on your site should have a clear next step. "Call Now for a Free Estimate." "Book Your Appointment." "Get a Quote in 60 Seconds." Make it obvious, make it easy, and repeat it more than once on the page.

A website without a call to action is like a salesperson who never asks for the sale. Friendly, informative, and completely useless.

4. Slow Loading Speed

Google has been clear about this: page speed is a ranking factor. A site that takes more than 3 seconds to load loses over half its visitors. And most of the slow sites we see on the Gulf Coast have the same culprits — enormous uncompressed images, cheap shared hosting, and bloated WordPress themes loaded with plugins nobody uses.

The fix: Compress your images, use modern hosting (Cloudflare Pages loads in under 1 second), and strip out anything you don't absolutely need. A fast, clean site beats a fancy slow one every time.

5. Outdated or Missing Business Information

Wrong hours. Old phone number. Services you don't offer anymore listed front and center. An address from two locations ago. This happens more than you'd think, especially with businesses that had a website built once and then forgot about it.

The fix: Audit your website quarterly. Better yet, use a platform like TurnkeyAI that gives you a client dashboard where you can update your own information and see changes go live the same day — no calling a web developer and waiting two weeks.
