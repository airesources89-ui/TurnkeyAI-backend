Our Story

Where It All Began

"A family is a circle of strength — every crisis faced together makes the circle stronger."

Robert and Eleanor Henderson settled in Canterbury, New Hampshire in the summer of 1952, newly married and full of hope. Robert worked the land while Eleanor built a home that became the heart of the community. Together they raised four children — Margaret, James, Ruth, and Thomas — who would go on to build families and lives across the country.

Three generations later, the Henderson name stretches from New Hampshire to Mississippi, from the mountains to the Gulf Coast. We gather every summer to remember where we came from and celebrate where we're going. This site is our way of staying connected in between.

Family History

Our Story Through the Years

1952
Robert & Eleanor Marry
Robert Henderson and Eleanor Mae Briggs wed at St. Andrew's Church in Canterbury on June 14th. 47 guests attended. The reception was held in the Henderson barn.
The Farm on Blakemore Road
Robert and Eleanor purchase 2.5 acres on Blakemore Road, building the farmhouse that would remain in the family for decades.
1955
1968
Four Children, One Household
By 1968 all four Henderson children — Margaret, James, Ruth, and Thomas — are living at home. Eleanor is known throughout Canterbury for her legendary Sunday suppers.
First Family Reunion
As the children begin moving away, the family holds its first official reunion at the Blakemore Road farm. 22 family members attend. A tradition is born.
1981
2012
Eleanor Passes
Eleanor Henderson passes peacefully at 84. The family gathers from six states to celebrate a woman who was the glue of four generations. Her recipes still make the rounds every Christmas.
This Website Goes Live
The Henderson family launches this site to keep the story alive and the family connected — no matter how far apart life takes everyone.
2025
